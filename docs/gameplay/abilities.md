Content preserved verbatim. This is V2 design material — hero abilities are not implemented in the v1 build described in [../versions/v1.md](../versions/v1.md). See [operators.md](operators.md) for the four Founding Operators built on this framework, and [../design/lore.md](../design/lore.md) for the world these rules originate from.

## WORLD RULES (Never Break)

WindArms is **NOT** fantasy.

WindArms is **NOT** magical.

WindArms is **NOT** superhero-based.

Every ability must be believable within the world's technology.

All powers originate from:

* Atmospheric pressure engineering
* Wind manipulation through advanced machinery
* Kinetic energy storage
* Storm energy harvesting
* Electromagnetic technology
* Mechanical engineering
* Aerodynamics
* Advanced materials

No spellcasting.
No glowing magical hands.
No elemental magic.
No summoned creatures.
No teleportation through unexplained means.

Every ability must feel engineered.

---

# GAMEPLAY PHILOSOPHY

Every operator should enhance player skill rather than replace it.

Abilities should create new movement, positioning, tactical, or environmental opportunities—not guarantee kills.

The player should still win primarily through:

* Aim
* Movement
* Positioning
* Teamwork
* Timing

Abilities are force multipliers, not win buttons.

---

# DESIGN REQUIREMENTS

For each operator, generate:

* Full name
* Codename
* Personality
* Backstory
* Visual design
* Armor concept
* Weapon preference
* Passive ability
* Tactical ability
* Ultimate ability
* Cooldowns
* Counterplay
* Team synergy
* Strengths
* Weaknesses
* Skill ceiling
* Sound design
* VFX direction
* Animation concepts
* HUD indicators
* Voice lines
* Lore connections
* Competitive balance analysis

---

# FINAL OBJECTIVE

Design four operators that are instantly recognizable, mechanically distinct, competitively balanced, and deeply connected to the world of WindArms. Their abilities should feel impossible in today's shooters yet completely logical within WindArms' atmospheric technology. Every operator should be enjoyable for both casual players and esports-level competitors, while establishing a timeless identity that can expand into future seasons without breaking the game's core philosophy.
