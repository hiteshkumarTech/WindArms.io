## Facing-direction — empirically proven, then corrected

`tools/blender/verify-kael-facing.py` — imports the **already-exported GLB** (not the FBX, so this proves what actually ships), places two orthographic cameras at world +Z and world −Z (glTF/runtime convention — Three.js's forward, not Blender's up axis), both looking at the character, and renders labeled PNGs. Axis mapping derived from Blender's own `export_yup=True` inverse conversion (`gltf_Z = -blender_Y`), spelled out in the script's header rather than asserted from memory.

**First run (pre-correction):**
| Render | Shows |
|---|---|
| `kael-view-from-plus-z.png` | **Face** — eyes, nose, mouth, chest emblem all visible |
| `kael-view-from-minus-z.png` | **Back of head/torso** |

**Result: Kael's visual forward = +Z. WindArms required forward = −Z. Correction required = yes.** This confirms the earlier math-based hypothesis, but as an actual observation of rendered pixels, not a repeat of the same reasoning.

**Correction applied** in `tools/blender/make-kael-source-glb.py`: a fixed 180° rotation around Blender's up axis (Z), applied to the armature and mesh **together** via the same `transform_apply` mechanism already used for the scale/rotation cleanup — never mesh-only, which would desync the armature modifier's bind reference from vertex data. GLB regenerated.

**Second run (post-correction), same script, same two camera positions:**
| Render | Shows |
|---|---|
| `kael-view-from-plus-z.png` | Back of head/torso |
| `kael-view-from-minus-z.png` | **Face** |

Inverted exactly as expected. Kael now faces **−Z**, matching the WindArms convention. Height (1.8300 m) and grounding (min-Z 0.000000) were re-measured after correction and are unchanged — a pure yaw, no distortion.

(Minor, non-blocking: the procedural floor/grid the verification script also adds didn't render visibly in either frame, likely camera framing. Doesn't affect the facing determination, which rests entirely on the character's own face-vs-back geometry.)

## Final classification: **ACCEPTED WITH LIMITATIONS**

Structurally, this rig is clean: 65 bones, single root, all required limb chains, all 10 finger chains, 100% weighted vertices, a passing deformation smoke test on all 4 sampled bones, and correct 1.83 m normalization with feet grounded at the origin. Forward direction is now empirically verified and corrected (see above) rather than left as an open question.

But **visual integration is not complete**, which is why this is not a plain ACCEPTED:

1. **No texture data.** Confirmed two independent ways — Blender's own `has_data: False` check, and the render itself showing flat missing-texture magenta. The character will render untextured until real texture data is sourced. Not a rig defect; a material/asset gap.
2. **Source geometry is high-poly by design** (299,915 tris) and requires the not-yet-built runtime derivative (`tools/make-kael-runtime.mjs`) before it's usable at any LOD budget — expected, not a regression, but real remaining work.

**This source is valid for pipeline and IK development, but not final visual presentation.** Do not treat the untextured asset as visually production-ready.

Both hands visibly track the weapon through hip-fire/ADS/movement/recoil/reload/inspect.

**Update (2026-07-22) — extended to `/v2/play`, unmodified.** The exact same `KaelFirstPersonArms` component is now also mounted in `V2PlayScene.tsx` — no play-specific fork, no changes to the arms system itself, confirming the component's original route-agnostic design. Kael's arms now track the weapon identically in both scenes. **Still not visually calibrated in either scene** (no browser access this session — a `?ik=1` dev tuner exists in `/v2/range` for a future pass to correct the elbow-pole/shoulder-offset/finger-curl first-pass defaults). Full current status: `docs/forge/operator-pipeline.md` §5's "Hand IK" note, `docs/design/weapons/vortex-rifle.md` §22b, and `docs/changelog.md`'s 2026-07-21/2026-07-22 entries.

**Update (2026-07-22, same day) — the arms mesh was mounted but invisible; found and fixed.** Manual browser validation caught what the prior structural checks couldn't: the mesh itself never appeared, despite IK/debug tooling working. Root cause was specific to how this ACCEPTED-WITH-LIMITATIONS asset is authored, not a new defect in it — its skeleton uses the same full-body "feet at origin" coordinate convention documented above for the body derivative, which a first-person arms consumer must explicitly recenter around (the original Step 4 arms-extraction/inspection pass had no reason to flag this, since it wasn't yet being mounted anywhere camera-relative). Fixed at the consumer level (`KaelFirstPersonArms.tsx`/`kaelArmRig.ts`), not by re-exporting or modifying the asset. Full root-cause trace: `docs/decisions.md`.

**Update (2026-07-22, later same day) — the asset itself DID need re-exporting after all: `operator-kael-arms.glb` rebuilt to fix a decimation artifact at the shoulder cut.** After the recentering fix above made the arms visible, manual browser testing found the geometry catastrophically deformed near the shoulder/upper-arm region — giant polygon slabs, not the clean derivative this gate originally accepted. Traced with real Blender automation against the same accepted rigged source used for this gate: the ORIGINAL extraction (Step 4, `tools/blender/make-kael-fp-arms.py`) is not at fault — its raw threshold-based selection produces uniformly tiny boundary edges (confirmed at every threshold tried). The defect is introduced by that SAME script's Decimate step, which was never given any protection at the open shoulder/torso cut boundary (only hand/finger regions are protected) — Collapse decimation next to an open topological edge stretched individual triangles to ~0.12–0.16m. Rebuilt with a new post-decimation cleanup step in the same script (delete long-edge faces, then clean up); measured 27x reduction in max triangle area on the real asset. A genuine independent bug in the boundary-loop classifier (present since this gate's original pass, never previously triggered) was found and fixed too. New hash/metrics recorded in `docs/decisions.md`'s "exploded geometry" rebuild entry; the ORIGINAL broken derivative was preserved (hash + inspector report, not committed) for comparison before being replaced. Still ACCEPTED WITH LIMITATIONS (same texture-data gap as always) — the geometric structure is now the thing this gate should be understood to cover, not the thing that silently regressed between this gate and the runtime mount work.

**Update (2026-07-22, same day, Step 6D) — the rebuilt asset itself checks out fine headlessly; the debug bounding box that made it LOOK broken was the bug.** Manual browser testing of the Step 6C rebuild reported the arms completely invisible with `IK disabled` on, plus a bounding-box overlay spanning most of the screen — read at face value, this looked like the rebuild had failed or introduced a new defect. Headless re-verification against this exact asset says otherwise: `THREE.Box3.setFromObject()` (the helper's method) reads only each mesh's static, load-time `geometry.boundingBox` and never reflects GPU-skinned deformation at all — proven by re-running the solver at IK weight 0 and weight 1 and getting an identical box either way. That alone explains an oversized, mispositioned overlay "spanning most of the screen" regardless of whether the real mesh nearby is rendering correctly. Fixed with `computeDeformedSkinnedBounds()`, which walks true per-vertex skinning math instead; against this same rebuilt asset it now reports `[1.009, 0.745, 0.195]` m, finite, arm-scale, nearest vertex 0.356m from camera — sane numbers, not evidence of a still-broken mesh. Whether the reported "completely invisible" symptom has a cause beyond this display bug remains open — a new solver-free "REST MESH DIAGNOSTIC" toggle was built specifically to answer that on the next manual check. This asset's ACCEPTED WITH LIMITATIONS status from the Step 6C update above is unchanged; nothing here required touching the GLB again. Full trace: `docs/decisions.md`.

**Update (2026-07-23, Step 6E) — resolved: the asset was never invisible, the REST pose just falls below the camera's frustum at first-person range; not an asset or rig defect.** Manual testing after the Step 6D fix above still reported invisible arms. Built a true camera-frustum classifier (`classifyVerticesInCameraSpace`) and ran it headlessly against THIS EXACT asset at every stage of the runtime transform chain: with the shipping camera offset, the rest pose (arms dangling at the sides — the same natural pose this gate's rig accepted, correct for third-person, never intended to be viewed from point-blank first-person range) places only 0.1% of the mesh's vertices inside the visible frustum. The SAME asset, driven by IK toward the actual grip target (the real shipping behavior), enters frame correctly and progressively (0.1% → 36.3% → 66.7% in-frustum at weight 0.25/0.5/1.0). Also directly disproved, against this asset specifically: backface culling from the Step 6C rebuild's decimation/scalpel pass (material is `THREE.DoubleSide`, confirmed by direct inspection — winding cannot hide any triangle regardless of what decimation did to it). This asset's structural classification (ACCEPTED WITH LIMITATIONS, texture-data gap only) is unchanged and now further reinforced — no further asset defect found after two full investigation passes (Step 6D and 6E) using independent methods. Full trace: `docs/decisions.md`.
